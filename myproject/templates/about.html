ABOUT ME

In August last year 2022, I attended the Time to Thrive Challenge organised by Dean Graziosi and Tony Robbins. I went all in and played full out!

After the challenge, I took up the offer to gain more knowledge and exposure in this industry that I was starting to fall in love with. So I purchased "Project Next" and committed to completing the course within a set number of days. After its completion, I was ushered into a new industry that was borderless—the coaching industry.

Before leaving Africa in 2019, I must admit that I had never heard about this industry. In my native Cameroon, when someone gave you directions on how to go about your life or business, they were considered a wise elder. Due to this traditional limitation inscribed in me through my upbringing and community values, I unconsciously fell into the imposter syndrome trap.

⦁ "I can't be a coach, I said to myself. I am only 33 and surely not old enough to be
considered an elder." Those words played over and over in my head.

However, whilst my mind was on a battlefield with ancient myths and my own limitations, there was a major shift happening around me.

On that day, whilst looking into the mirror, I was able to remove myself from the moment and look at my life journey from the outside in. Based on the African standard and in a healthy competitive relationship with my peers, I had achieved a lot. And despite losing my dad at 16 ️

I had always wanted an MBA before I turned 30, so I applied to a few universities and got my first admission into the German University of Cairo in Egypt.

So I resigned from my role as finance manager and relocated. I lived in Egypt for 2 years studying business management MBA, before getting another offer to study at Coventry University in London.

While preparing for my interview, I found out I was pregnant with my second son and that I may have to choose between nurturing my newborn and my Global Business MBA course.
⦁ I didn't want to give up either, so I pushed through the process.


Just after I got my visa, my son was born, and I faced the hardest decision of my life. Leave him at 3 months old or forgo the MBA.

⦁ I faced backlash for even contemplating leaving him at such a tender age. He was going to be raised by my mom, the same woman who raised me, so why was it such a hard decision to make? Well, it turns out I got on that plane and left.

⦁ Did I mention that 7 days after my son was born, I had a launch day for my new restaurant project, which turned out to be a very successful venture, at least while I was on the ground?

⦁ I landed in London in January of 2019 with my t-shirt covered in breast milk from my licky boobs. My newborn was looking for mommy, and mommy was in another part of the world.

Because of that traumatic separation, I vowed to make baby Kuden proud. I limited myself to two activities: studies and part-time work.

12 months later, I crushed my MBA with 10 As and got a distinction degree.

Instead of transitioning into a blissful career, COVID 19 ruined my plans to secure a good job.

First the furlough and then the lockdowns.. dear oh dear..

By this time, both my kids had joined me in the UK. I found myself in uncharted territory; locked up inside a tight British home with 2 young boys was no fun.

Hours turned into days, and days into weeks. Stay home the government reiterated.

Something unexpected happened. Frustrations turned into tears, and tears into depression, and depression into suicide. You read that right. I no longer wanted to be here.

But God had a plan for my life.

⦁ With the help of a coach whom I found randomly whilst watching self-help YouTube videos, I picked myself up and started a business with my mobile phone and from home.

My overall portfolio has expanded, and I am amazed by my results. Here's how my $2500 initial investment has become the gift that keeps giving:

⦁ Being able to pay for coaching programmes for my continuous growth.

⦁ It funded my sister's travel from Cameroon to London and pays for her petroleum and chemical engineering tuition at a reputable university in London.

⦁ I bought some stocks on the stock market, which I plan to hold for a few years.

⦁ I wrote and self-published my first book, translated into french, Spanish and German, has sold over 100 copies and received some heartwarming and amazing feedback.

I couldn't have done much without the first coach I came across, if I hadn't believed in myself, and if I hadn't started my business.

So despite achieving all these things, why was I still feeling unworthy?

Then I realised it truly was FEAR and not even a lack of self-confidence. I have always been a confident person, so somewhere I was afraid of the unknown.

But guess what? I didn't let that stop me.
